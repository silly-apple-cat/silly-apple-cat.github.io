Where did EDM begin?

Whilst the genre of EDM may seem new, it actually has been around since the 1970s, and has continued to develop over the years.

1970’s Disco and Synthpop Electronic Dance Music

The beginning of EDM came from the disco genre, which was very popular in the late 1970s. Disco music uses drum machines and electronic instruments to create synthesized rhythms.

Some songs which helped to create the EDM scene include the 1977 synthesized disco hit "I Feel Love" by Pete Bellotte and Giorgio Moroder, and "Rock Your Baby" by George McCrae.

Synthpop, Post-Disco and Electro Music of the 1980s

Synth music, or synthpop, was developed alongside house music and electro music during the "post-disco" era in the 1980s. As technology advanced, more music of this genre was created.

Music produced during this time include "Take On Me" by a-ha, and also "On and On" by Jesse Saunders.

1990’s: The Beginning of Contemporary Electronic Dance Music Sounds

There were a variety of new EDM genres which were created around this time, which include techno music, house music, hardcore rave (hardstyle), dub, trance, and drum and bass.

Music that was produced during this time include songs such as "Around the World" by Daft Punk, and "Halcyon" by Orbital.

From Popular to Mainstream: EDM in the 2000s

Electronic dance music, despite its slow start, eventually picked up popularity in the western world thanks to international DJs and music producers drawing attention to the genre.

From there, we began to see producers such as Daft Punk, Skrillex and Marshmello, who still make EDM music to this day.
